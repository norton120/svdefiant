When I was wedged in the forward berth locker pulling [new nav light cable](/blog/let-there-be-light) yesterday, I paused to be annoyed by the dangling fan cord across the locker door. I installed the fan last year temporarily to see if it would help circulate the HVAC into the rest of the cabin (it really does). Finishing it off was never a priority because it worked, and _Defiant_ has no shortage of more important things to fix.

{{< figure src="before-out.jpg" >}}
{{< figure src="before-in.jpg" caption="annoying cable dangling and a big chunk of neoprene insulation to keep the fan from rattling. Functional? Yep. Pretty? Nope." >}}

But I had a few minutes while the heat shrink on the new spade connectors cooled, perfect time to clean this up. It wasn't much work at all. I drilled a tiny hole, fed the cable back, added a few zip wire braces to route it at the top edge of the closet inside, and then made a pair of y connectors for the back of the existing lamp power. Then I traced and cut the neoprene so it fit the back of the fan instead of a big ugly chunk of sponge. 

{{< figure src="after.jpg" caption="all tucked away and visually clean, and no more wire to catch in the door or ratty foam block" >}}


Fixing it wasn't a big deal, it took me an extra twenty minutes tops. Each one of these little fixes is a tiny chip away at making _Defiant_ a magnificent vessel again. The best way to eat an elephant is still one little bite at a time.
