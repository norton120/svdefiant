The week before you actually leave is different from all the weeks before it. The big refit items are done or accepted. What's left is the pile of deferred things that have been sitting there since you bought the boat, waiting for a reason to finally get handled. This was that week.

### The Engine Deserved Better

The Yanmar has been running fine, but the maintenance on it was borderline embarrassing. First order of business: a proper oil change. Not the top-off kind — a real drain-and-refill. I bought an extractor pump to make it clean and easy, which naturally didn't work, so back it went and out came the drain plug and a bucket. Takes longer, but the oil is out and fresh stuff is in.

While I was already in the bilge cursing, I pulled the fuel filters. The Racor primary was a crime scene — actual mud in the bowl, the kind that makes you genuinely wonder what the previous owners were burning and where they were getting it. Drained it, swapped the element, refilled with clean diesel. The secondary got the same treatment. The engine runs the same, but I feel considerably better about it.

Last engine item was a checklist: ATF level in the transmission, v-belt tension, oil. Simple stuff, but the things most likely to strand you if you blow through without checking. It's laminated now and mounted inside the engine cover. The Yanmar doesn't really give second chances on the v-belt.

### The Staysail Finally Showed Up

I dropped the staysail at Evolution weeks ago. Every time I followed up it was going to be ready "by end of week" — which, if you've been following along, is exactly the kind of promise that should be taken as entertainment rather than information. But it's back now, and it's installed. Getting it hanked on and the halyard sorted with the new forestay geometry took some messing around, but she's flying right.

To go with it, the fairlead blocks for the staysail and jib sheets are in and rigged. Running the sheets without proper leads is fine at the dock and annoying underway. That problem is gone.

### The Anchor Roller That Wouldn't Install Itself

The second anchor roller has been sitting on the cabin sole since April. Every week I walked past it and thought about how I should bolt it down. This week I bolted it down. Drilled through the bowsprit, backed it with a stainless plate, and now the second hook lives on deck where it belongs instead of on the floor where I trip on it.

Propane tanks are filled — big tank and the grill tanks both.

---

Defiant is a lot closer to ready than she was a week ago. The engine is sorted, both headsails are properly rigged and sheeted, and the ground tackle is squared away. What's next is actually leaving.
